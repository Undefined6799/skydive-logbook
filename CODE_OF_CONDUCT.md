[Contributor Covenant 3.0](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/code_of_conduct/)

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation
in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless
of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open,
welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Encouraged behaviours

Behaviours that contribute to a positive environment for our community
include, but are not limited to:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility and apologising to those affected by our
  mistakes, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community.

## Restricted behaviours

The following behaviours are restricted in our community spaces. They
are not always grounds for immediate action but, when persistent,
unwelcome, or escalating, will be addressed by community leaders:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or
  political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email
  address, without their explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in
  a professional setting.

## Critical and prohibited behaviours

The following behaviours are never acceptable. A single incident is
grounds for immediate action up to and including a permanent ban from
the community:

- Threats of violence of any kind, against any person or group.
- Incitement of violence toward any individual, including encouraging
  a person to commit suicide or to engage in self-harm.
- Sexual exploitation of any kind, particularly involving minors.
- Deliberate, knowing publication of private communications or
  private personal information ("doxxing") without the affected
  person's express consent.
- Knowingly false, malicious, or bad-faith reporting that misuses this
  Code of Conduct.

## Reporting an incident

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of
Conduct, please report it.

**Contact:** `[REPORTING ADDRESS]`

When you report, please include:

1. Your contact information (so we can follow up with you).
2. Names (real, usernames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved.
3. A description of what happened, when, and where (which community
   space, e.g. an issue, a discussion thread).
4. Any additional context, including links or screenshots if you have
   them.

All reports will be handled with discretion. We are committed to
protecting the privacy of reporters and, to the extent possible,
respondents.

## Addressing and repairing harm

In place of a fixed "enforcement ladder," Contributor Covenant 3.0
adopts a *restorative* posture: when a violation is identified,
community leaders ask what happened, who was harmed, and what is
needed to repair the harm. The response is calibrated to the incident,
guided by the principles below.

When a community leader determines that a violation has occurred, the
typical response will include some combination of:

1. **A private conversation** with the person whose behaviour caused
   harm, identifying the specific behaviour and its impact, and
   making clear what is expected going forward.
2. **A public or private acknowledgement** of the harm to those
   affected, with the affected parties' consent.
3. **A defined repair**, where appropriate — e.g., editing or
   removing a hurtful comment, an apology, a temporary or permanent
   restriction on participation in specific community spaces.
4. **A duration of restricted participation**, where the conduct
   warrants it. The duration is proportionate to the severity and
   pattern of behaviour.
5. **A permanent removal** from the community for behaviour that is
   critical or prohibited under this Code of Conduct, or where prior
   restorative steps have been disregarded.

Community leaders will explain their reasoning when communicating
decisions to the people involved.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — issues,
pull requests, discussions, the project's chat presences if any —
and also when an individual is officially representing the community
in public spaces (e.g., a conference talk identifying themselves as a
project maintainer). Representing the community includes using an
official email address, posting via an official social-media account,
or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline
event.
